Chapter 3:The Meeting

It was Saturday, the 27, the weekend after Thanksgiving, and the last one before December descended on Mwayi. Peter and Mwayi were set to meet at the local library’s cafe for an agreement of terms and to come up with the "cover story." Even the thought of a cover story made Mwayi laugh, because it made her realize that at what her mom would call "her big age," she cared so much about people’s opinions that she would go through the trouble of concocting an entire scheme to deceive her loved ones.

When Peter got to the cafe, he texted Mwayi, who let him know she had arrived 10 minutes prior to him, "I just arrived! Where are you sitting?" She promptly responded, "I am sitting to your left, with the green and white flowery dress and jean jacket." When he turned to his left, he saw the most beautiful woman he had ever set his eyes on getting up from the last booth against the wall. He immediately shoots his cousin, Mphatso, a text. "You did not warn me your friend was this gorgeous. I fear I may be the one owing you the favor after this." Before he could look up from his text, he heard the most angelic voice say, "Hi, you must be Peter." He knew when he heard that voice, if the context of their meeting was not enough, that he was in for a whirlwind of trouble.
