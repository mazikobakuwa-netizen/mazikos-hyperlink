Chapter 0.3: Artist Statement

This short story was inspired by the assignment prompting for a narrative with hyperlinks, as well as my rediscovered love for reading more as a new year’s resolution, and the recurring topic of discussion in my ethnic home as graduation approaches. I took the narrative approach a bit differently than my classmates did, using HTML to create a short story. The discussion of marriage is prevalent in my community, with joy before the age of 28, and with shame after the age of 30. Once someone of marrying age has passed an "appropriate" age to get married, they are often the brunt of sarcasm, jokes, unsolicited prayer topics, and more. It forces young girls to get married before their frontal lobes are fully developed at 25, resulting in more mistaken or unhappy marriages and divorces from our generation, who refuse to stay as the former generations of women did. While marriage is a good thing to aspire to, works of art in the form of spoken words, speeches, or books, by artists like Chimamanda Ngozie or Ola Awonubi, teach us that there is more to life than marriage. Successful careers are a good thing for women to feel proud about, without feeling as though they are a hindrance on the other courses of life. I hope this short quirky story has enlightened you about the plights of "overripe mangos," who are just proudly successful women who should be uplifted.
