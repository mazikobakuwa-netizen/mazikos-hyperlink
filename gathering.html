Chapter 1: The Gathering

The holidays remained Mwayi's most dreaded time. Family members, both far and wide, near and overseas, travelled to their family compound for Thanksgiving, Christmas, and New Year's. It was not the holidays themselves that set her spirits sour, but her family members. They come bearing gifts, and their opinions about her marital status, opinions masked in sarcasm, mean jokes, faux concern, and disappointment.

As gifts were being opened and shared, Mwayi's aunt Alinane made a snide comment to Mwayi's cousin, Mphatso, about how she would have expected her sister's gift for Christmas to be a son-in-law, saying, " I would have expected a nephew-in-law to be by her side this year for Thanksgiving dinner, but what else can I expect. I just pray my sister gets her wish. Well, better luck for Mwayi next year." Mwayi tried to ignore comments like this, as they became her reoccuring reality from the age of 25, but after 8 years, a person can only take so much. She suddenly finds herself blurting out, "Well then, thank God my boyfriend will be joining us for New Year's!" Astonished, her family all snapped their heads to the voice of the dramatic outburst, finding that it was Mwayi who they least expected to bring anyone home.
