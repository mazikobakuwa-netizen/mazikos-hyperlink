Chapter 5: The Showstopper of an Announcement

"Myra’s speech made me realize she is right. When she saw us in the library, it was a display of love, which at this point, some would find premature, but true love. Over the short course of time Mwayi and I have known each other, I have realized there is no one I would rather spend my time with. Every laugh shared with her was more real than the last, and every skipped heartbeat I felt was louder than the last. I cannot imagine spending my life with anyone else." As Peter went on, Mwayi swore she was in a dream as she thought, "This was not a part of the script! What is he doing? Oh my goodness, we are going to need so much more rehearsal, and I am going to end him for this. Oh my goodness, I wonder if Nafe knows!" Myra then snapped out of her daydream as she heard her mother scream, "YES! SHE WILL!" Uncle Bo said, "Yoh, the women in this family will be the end of me. Nane, that is Mwayi’s line! You cannot answer for her. Mwayi, don’t keep us waiting, spare the poor guy’s knees. Will you marry the chap or not?" Mwayi suddenly snapped out of her thoughts to realize that her best friend Nafe was recording the moment, who had come out of nowhere, but really snuck in mid proposal speech when she realized her cue was much sooner than she and Peter had planned. See, the truth is, this part was not staged. Over the last 4 weeks, Peter had been gushing to Nafe after every meeting. He couldn’t get over how perfect he found Mwayi to be. He eventually concluded the Sunday before this Wednesday's New Year’s Eve Party that he had to make things official, as he could not imagine Mwayi having to do this constantly to prove herself to people who made her feel like she was not enough, while he felt like she was spectacular. He concocted a sideline plan with Nafe, who was more than ecstatic to be apart, to propose in front of Mwayi’s entire family. He realized the only thing that mattered was the future he wanted to get to know her more, not their background.
