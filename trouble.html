Chapter 4: I Smell Trouble

It was the evening of Mwayi’s family's New Year’s Party, which had her scrambling and frantic to look her best. Her meeting with Peter was successful and even fruitful, as she realized how handsome and charming he was upon meeting him. Over the course of the next 4.5 weeks, they agreed to meet 3 times a week and rehearse their history, so their storylines matched. They agreed on the following facts

  • They met through a work partnership, for a project that Mwayi was heading for Peter’s company, Northern Star Inc., which focused on AI-powered Business Research.
  • Mwayi was tasked with advising on strategies for product marketing after Peter’s company lost a major government contract.
  • They met in November of the prior year, where they felt it was too soon for him to meet her family that Winter, so they agreed on him meeting them this Winter.
  • Her favorite color was turquoise. His favorite color was light shades of brown, such as nude and tan (which was reflected in his clothing choices, as he met her in a white button-up covered by a tan quarter zip sweater and navy blue slacks with a loose but fitted cuff at the ankles, matched with khaki suede loafers.
  • He was 36, turning 37 in March of the following year, where they planned to go to Turks and Caicos for a resort vacation, which was a complementary age gap to her 32 years of age.
  • He was born and raised in Lilongwe, Malawi, while she was born and raised in Blantyre ( a 5 hour drive, which explains why they had not met until now). He then relocated to Cambridge, where his company is now based, similar to Mwayi’s office location, after graduating with a Master’s in Data Administration from the Polytechnic University of Malawi. Mwayi also relocated after graduating from the University of Lusaka in Zambia, as opportunities for her field were rare in Malawi.
Knowing all of this, they felt confident they could answer any questions that may come along.

Many were dancing and singing, with tunes of Tyla and TxC mixes running through the air. The younger kids were trying to get Mwayi to make dancing videos to the newest trends with them, but she was content sitting in the corner sipping her 4th glass of champagne to calm her nerves. Peter, on the other hand, was in an intense game of Spades with Mwayi’s age-mate cousins, uncles, and dad. The men passed around beers provided by the ladies, constantly checking on their needs as they were in the garage. They claimed to need intense focus to be a victor, but Peter figured it was a ploy to get away from the loud gossiping wives, bored teens taking social media-worthy pictures, and the running and dancing children. The oldest aunt in the family, and next to be the matriarch once Amama(mother) /agogo (grandma) passed on, Aunt Maggie, interrupted the men right before the most dramatic victory could be enacted by Mwayi’s cousin, Chimwemwe. "Men, enough with all this! Come join your family for our toast and New Year’s countdown so we can reflect together." Chimz exclaimed, "Aunty, really? I was finally about to end Uncle Bo’s winning streak!" Aunt Maggie replied, "There will be more than enough time for that in the new year."

As the men joined the clan in the living room, Peter’s arms found their way around Mwayi’s waist. "I could get used to this… but that is crazy. I have only known her for one month, but every time we met and rehearsed, I found myself seeing the real her, in the mistakes of her scripts and the way her nose scrunched when she laughed. Even the way she banteringly ate at my take-out food on our late nights, so we were more natural acting in front of her family," thought Peter. Mwayi couldn’t help but lean her body deeper into his warm embrace, inhaling the musky scent fused by Peter’s cologne. From the other end of the room, Mwayi’s cousin, and nemesis, who could not stand the success and independence Mwayi had, Myra watched this interaction. She could not wait until it was her turn to share her reflection, so she could share what she knew from watching and eavesdropping on their first meeting at the library.

As Myra’s turn came around, Mwayi rolled her eyes at how dramatically she chinked her glass of champagne with her spoon and cleared her throat to get the attention of the teenagers who could not stop snickering at a video they watched. " As we close out this year, I have so much to reflect on. Especially on the love I have seen and experienced, like that of my dear cousin, Mwayi’s, who I even had the pleasure of watching their romance in the library the other day, and just knowing-," Before Myra could finish her sentence, Peter drew the attention to him and a tensely fearful Mwayi, by exclaiming, "I have an announcement.
